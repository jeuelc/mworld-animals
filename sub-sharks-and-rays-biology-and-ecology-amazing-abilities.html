Imagine having super powers like being able to smell or 'feel' someone from hundreds of metres away. These super powers are the norm for sharks and rays as they navigate their way through the oceans looking for prey.

Sharks and rays possess an incredible sense of smell. They are able to detect one drop of blood in the equivalent of a household swimming pool! Not to worry though, detecting a drop of blood from kilometres away is a popular myth...busted!

'Feeling' prey is crucial, especially when you can't see them and they're not bleeding. By using what's called a 'lateral-line' system, sharks can detect pressure changes and vibrations from struggling animals in the water within 100 metres.

Even cooler is that sharks and rays can detect electrical signals, like those from your heartbeat! This is particularly useful, especially if your food is hiding in the seagrass or buried under sand.
