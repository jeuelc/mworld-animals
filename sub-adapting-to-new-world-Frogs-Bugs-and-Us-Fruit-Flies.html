Fruit Flies

 
click here

Species that are widespread often have to cope with living in a diverse range of climates. Some body types are better suited to particular regions than others. In Europe, fruit flies that live in cold areas generally have larger wings than those in warmer regions.

When the species was first introduced to North America there was no relationship between wing size and the climate (or latitude) it lived in. But over the following two decades the flies rapidly adapted to their new environment and eventually the same European pattern became evident in North America. Thus, there appears to be strong selection for fruit flies to have wing sizes that best suit the climates that they are living in.
