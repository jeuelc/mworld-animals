Soapberry Bug

 
click here click here

Having a big mouth can often get you into trouble. But for the soapberry bug in eastern Australia, having big mouthparts has been a key to its recent success. It feeds on fruit, but needs to be able to pierce the outer capsule to reach the seeds below. Its beak was long enough to do this when it fed upon fruits from the native woolly rambutan tree.

Several decades ago the balloon vine was introduced to the area. Its fruit are much larger, and initially the soapberry bug was unable to pierce the outer capsule to reach its seeds. Over the space of 30 or 40 years the soapberry bug has been able to evolve the size of its beak so that it can now feed on the balloon vine fruits.
