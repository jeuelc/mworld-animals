Importance of the Food Web

 
click here click here

Sharks and rays help maintain healthy oceans by playing a crucial role in regulating the marine food web. As top predators in their ecosystems, they help keep populations of other animals in check and prevent the food-web falling into disarray. By removing sharks, we may also see the disappearance of other animals.

In Hawaii, tiger sharks help protect seagrass which provides areas for other small fish and invertebrates to breed. How? Well, when tiger sharks move in to hunt sea turtles, sea turtles (which eat seagrass) are forced to move out. This means they don't over-feed on any one area and remove all the seagrass.

In Chesapeake Bay, USA, overfishing of large, predatory sharks was linked to a collapse in the local shellfish population!
