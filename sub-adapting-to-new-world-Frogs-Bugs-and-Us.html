Frogs, Bugs and Us

Adaptation and evolution are generally seen as things that happen over long periods of time, perhaps even millions of years. While changes in animals can be observed in fossils we dig up, they can also change much faster than that. We call such changes that occur within our lifetime (but over several generations for the animal) 'contemporary evolution'. Charles Darwin used evidence of contemporary evolution in pigeons and domestic animals to support his theory of natural selection. But contemporary evolution can also be observed in animals as diverse as frogs, bugs, and even humans!
