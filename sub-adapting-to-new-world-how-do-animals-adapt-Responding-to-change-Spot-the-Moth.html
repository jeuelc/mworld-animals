How many moths can you see on this tree? Which one is easier to spot? They are the same species but their DNA differs by one mutation.

Birds like to eat moths and have an easy time spotting black moths on grey trees. In the 1700s, most of these so called peppered moths in England were mottled salt-and-pepper coloured, if you like. Their dark brothers and sisters did not escape the hungry birds in the forest.

In the 1800s, factories nearby produced a lot of soot which coated the trees. This meant that the dark moths became almost invisible and multiplied, while the previously camouflaged salt-and-pepper moths became easy prey for birds.

In the last 50 years we have used more advanced factories, which produce less pollution. If you're a black moth, you'll need to fly away!
