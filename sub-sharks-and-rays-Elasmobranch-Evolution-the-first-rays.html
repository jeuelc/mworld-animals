The First Rays

150 million years ago, in the late Jurassic Period, some sharks started to live mostly on the sea floor and evolved into some of the modern rays we see today. Most rays have flattened, disc-like bodies with pectoral fins that are fused to the head, making them look like wings. There are some exceptions to the general shape of a ray such as the guitarfish family which look half-shark and half-ray!

Among the rays, we have some strange-looking and electrifying species...

The electric rays have an organ near their pectoral fins that is able to produce an electric shock to stun their prey!

The sawfish have a long nose-like projection that looks exactly like a chainsaw! They thrash this around in the water to literally dice up fish to eat. Their saw is so effective that some island tribes have used their long 'nose' as a sword!
