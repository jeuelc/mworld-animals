Thresher Shark

Thresher sharks are famous for their really, really long tail. Threshers reach up to 5.5 metres long (a lot of this is tail length) and they use their tail like a whip to stun fish so they can capture them.

There are actually three species of thresher shark: the common 'Alopias vulpinus', the pelagic, 'Alopias pelagicus', and the bigeye, 'Alopias superciliosus'.

Not a lot is known about the thresher shark but they do have some interesting behaviour. Threshers seem to like to spend the day in the cold depths, down 300 - 500 metres, and at night come up and spend their time at the surface to hunt for food. Because the temperature change from the depths to the surface can be dramatic and can shift by up to 20 degrees Celsius, the thresher uses a special circulatory system to keep its blood warm and protect its eyes and brain from shock. Imagine if they didn't have that - brain-freeze!
