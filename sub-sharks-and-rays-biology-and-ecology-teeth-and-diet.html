Teeth and Diet

If you were a shark or ray, you'd never have to go to the dentist and wouldn't even have a problem crushing the hardest of sweets! Sharks and rays have teeth that never run out; they grow and replace themselves constantly over their lifetimes. Some are replaced every 14 days!

The shape of their teeth is distinctive and is often used to identify one species from another. They can even differ between a male and female! The shape of teeth fall into three main types and they are especially designed for their diet;

- Grinding plates: most rays and some sharks use these to help grind the hard shells of invertebrates like crabs and shellfish.

- Triangular/blade-cutting: these are used for shearing and cutting large chunks of flesh from large prey, like mammals and turtles.

- Long, fang-like: these are used to seize and hold prey, such as fish, so that they can be swallowed whole.
