Sharks are victims of us more than we are of them. Although we are captivated by these majestic animals, our fear can take over. Newspapers and TV often paint them as merciless killers, prompting people to go out and hunt for the 'killers'.

Sharks are perhaps more curious about us than we are about them. Since they have no hands, sharks often test or inspect items like boats or cages with a gentle bite. However, this 'testing' bite for most humans is damaging and often ends in the loss of limbs or life.

What we must remember is that many 'attacks' are not from hunger but probably curiosity. Most deaths are from blood loss rather than being eaten. Sharks are dangerous and should be respected as such, but do you think hunting them because of fear is right? They're an animal simply going about their business in their domain.
