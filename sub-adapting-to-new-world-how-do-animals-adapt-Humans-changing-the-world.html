Humans Changing the World

Humans are just one of millions of species that live on Earth, but our impact on the world is profound. We clear habitats to build our cities, and mine valuable rocks from the earth to build our houses, businesses and roads. Our daily lives result in the pollution of the air, water and landscape. This not only changes our world, but also the environments available for animals to live in.

Some of these environments like skyscrapers - are weird and wonderful, and many animals like rats and pigeons have adapted and manage to thrive in these very unnatural habitats.

Many human-induced changes are so sudden and severe that affected animals have no chance of surviving. For example when we chop down or burn jungle to make way for roads or farms, animals perish. If that is the animal's only habitat, they face extinction. In the case of global warming, the effect is much slower. Even so, a number of animal species cannot adapt fast enough.
