The speed at which animals adapt is not simply a matter of time. It's a matter of generations. A generation is the time it takes an animal to become an adult and produce babies of its own. In most animals, producing offspring provides an opportunity to reshuffle the DNA recipe by merging the mother's and the father's DNA.

Insects generally have a short generation time, lasting only days, weeks or months. Humans, dogs and cats have much longer generation times that last many years. This means that some animals may be able to respond to environmental change much faster than others.
