Tiger Shark

 
click here click here

Tiger sharks,'Galeocerdo cuvier', are named for their tiger-like stripes. They can grow up to six metres long. They live in the warmer tropical and sub-tropical waters of the world. They're also common around the Pacific islands.

Tiger sharks usually hunt during the night in shallow waters and eat a lot of foods including fish, crustaceans, dolphins, turtles and birds. They are sometimes called the 'garbage bin of the sea' because they eat so many things; people have even found car licence plates, tyres and baseballs in their stomachs!

Because of their wide choice of food and love of shallow waters, tiger sharks are considered especially dangerous to humans. In Hawaii there is an average of four attacks per year, which thankfully are rarely fatal. Amazingly, the attack rate is very low considering the hundreds of people that use the water each day.
