Adapting to New Worlds

Have you heard of the dodo? This large flightless bird was last spotted in 1662. It is now extinct.

Animals spend their lives looking for food, avoiding being eaten themselves, and finding others to reproduce with. This is hard enough as it is, but survival is made even more difficult when their environment changes. Can you imagine trying to learn a musical instrument when the instrument, the music you are playing and your teacher change every month? Plants, microscopic organisms and animals need to adapt as the world around them changes. These changes often occur over millions of years, but can also happen much faster than that, sometimes over a handful of human generations!

As we humans affect the world through engineering, food production and pollution, animals are having to adapt to survive.
