Fishing, Finning and the Future

There are seven billion people on Earth and our demand on the ocean for food has never been greater. Our current rate of fishing exceeds the abilities of sharks and rays to reproduce and consequently, their populations have fallen on average by 90%!

Sharks and rays are often caught accidentally. Alive or dead, they are usually released because their flesh is worth little money. However, their fins have enormous value in the Asian markets. In some cases the fins are chopped off while the animals are still alive before they're thrown back to die in the sea. 'Finning' is threatening shark populations and several countries have made finning illegal.

Many countries have invested in research, adopted methods and made laws to reduce the numbers of sharks and rays caught in commercial fisheries. While these animals can pose a threat to humans in some circumstances, we all need to work together to protect the shark and ray populations and ensure the health of our oceans.
