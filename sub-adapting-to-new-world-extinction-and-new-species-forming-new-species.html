Forming New Species

Members of the same species are able to reproduce with each other to produce healthy offspring. Members of different species either cannot reproduce, or do not produce young that can reproduce further. How are new species formed? How for example - have we ended up with zebras and horses?

The answer generally comes down to separation or isolation. Individuals that hang out together are more likely to mate, and their genotypes never really get a chance to diverge too far from each other. But when animals from different areas never meet - perhaps due to a river or mountain separating them - their genotypes gradually become more distinct as well. Speciation is responsible for the great diversity of life that we have on Earth.

While we are encountering new species daily, these species did not originate the previous day. It's just that there is so much left to explore and discover in our world!
