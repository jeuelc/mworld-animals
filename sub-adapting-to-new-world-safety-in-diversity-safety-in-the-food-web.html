Species don't live in isolation. They interact with other species in the same area. Plants use the sun's energy and nutrients in the soil to grow. They in turn get eaten by animals, which may then get eaten by other, larger animals. These 'food webs' may be simple, involving only a handful of species, or more complex and involve dozens of species. The larger, more complex food webs are often more stable and resilient to environmental change or the addition or removal of a species. This is why having a large and diverse number of species in an area (or 'biodiversity') can be important for the proper functioning of our environment. High levels of biodiversity may therefore provide safety in numbers.
