Noisy Frogs

 
click here

Have you ever tried to have a conversation in a noisy room? Either you have to shout loudly to be heard by the other person, or move somewhere else to continue the conversation. Frogs that communicate with each other using sound are having a similar problem in our increasingly noisy world. Frogs use their calls to let other individuals know where they are, or to locate potential mates.

Those frogs living near noisy roads or airports have been adapting the way that they call and communicate with each other. Some species stop calling during noisy periods, while others do the opposite and call more and at a higher pitch to be heard above the racket. To persist in these human environments, frogs need to change the way that they croak.
