Great White Shark

The great white shark,'Carcharodon carcharias', is named after its distinctive two-toned colouration and is the most famous of all sharks. They can measure four to six metres long and weigh more than 2000 kilograms, the size of a minibus! Even their pups are huge, measuring just over one metre and weighing about 35 kilograms.

Great whites show enormous learning ability and rely on experience to attack fast and clever seals. Their teeth are specialised for tearing the tough skin of seals and whales. Triangular, serrated teeth act like a giant saw as the head shakes from side to side to tear off large chunks of flesh.

Great whites can travel extraordinary distances. One shark was found to have travelled from South Africa to Australia (and back!), a distance of more than 15,000 kilometres, in less than nine months!
