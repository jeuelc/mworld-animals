xtinction is a natural part of life. 99% of all animal species that have ever lived have become extinct: dinosaurs, woolly mammoths, the dodo, the gastric brooding frog and millions more. Once an animal becomes extinct, it is gone forever. So what causes extinction? It occurs when animals are unable to adapt quickly enough to the changes in their world. These changes may be natural, such as a new predator that arrives in your area or a large storm that destroys your home. Or it can be as a result of human activities, including hunting animals for food or clearing the habitat in which they live. Some species are more vulnerable, such as those that live in a small area, have a fussy diet, are large, or only produce a small number of babies.

New species originate and old species become extinct continually. Scientists have not yet managed to create new species or resurrect extinct ones. We humans need to ensure that we don't accelerate the rate of animal extinction if we want to preserve biodiversity on our planet.
