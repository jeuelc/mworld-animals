Sharks and Human Cultures

 
click here click here click here/

Although sharks can seem to be creatures of nightmares, killing almost anything in their sight, they have a unique place in human culture. Even today, particularly in the island nations of the world, sharks are worshipped and deeply rooted in their culture and lifestyle.

Island nations tend to rely almost entirely on the ocean for food and they recognised early in their history the importance of sharks to the ecosystem. In Polynesian culture, sharks are considered gods and spirits of their ancestors, who protect both the islands and their people. Although they are also fished for food, it is considered bad luck to catch or kill a shark without proper preparation. Fishermen have to go through a sacred ritual days before they fish and have to pray to the shark spirit. This is to create a respectful connection between shark and man and to ensure there will be more sharks in the future.
