The First Sharks

The oldest known shark fossil is that of 'Cladoselace' and it was so well preserved that there were traces of skin, muscle and kidneys! It had a streamlined body, seven gills, two dorsal fins with spines, a pair of broad pectoral fins on its sides, forward-facing eyes and a mouth at the front of the head, instead of underneath like modern sharks. Over the next 200 million years, shark evolution exploded into a variety of weird and wonderful forms. Here are some of the stranger ones...

Stethacanthus with a flattened forehead and a modified fin that looked like an ironing board. Helicoprion with a row of teeth that curled out of the bottom of its mouth and extended as the shark grew. Falcatus with a forward-facing spike on top of its head!
