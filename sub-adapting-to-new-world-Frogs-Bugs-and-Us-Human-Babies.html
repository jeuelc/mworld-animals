Human knowledge and technology has affected the course of our own evolution. Until the 20th century, babies that were too small or too large were unlikely to survive. Small babies that were born too soon (called 'premature') were sometimes too weak to survive after birth, while large babies couldn't be born naturally and required an operation on the mother called a 'Caesarean section'. A few hundred years ago, a mother might die if her baby was too big to pass through the birth canal or if there were other complications. In some cases the baby died too. This meant that only DNA that produced medium-sized babies was likely to be passed on to future generations.

Advances in medical technology have resulted in more premature babies surviving, and larger babies being born by Caesarean sections. In some countries this has led to a greater diversity in the size of newborns surviving. We are the only animal on Earth that has managed to harness technology to relax the intensity of natural selection. This makes us very adaptable!
