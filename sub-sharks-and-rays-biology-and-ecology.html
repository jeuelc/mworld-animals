Biology and Ecology

Sharks and rays occupy all the oceans of the world, from the ice-cold depths to the balmy waters of the tropics. As top predators, they help maintain healthy oceans by regulating the marine food web. Despite their importance, they are mysterious creatures and we still know very little about them.

What we do know about sharks and rays is that they are highly specialised animals displaying a range of strategies that have made them successful over millions of years. Sharks and rays vary in how they give birth. Some species lay eggs and others give birth to live young.

Each species can often be identified by their teeth alone, as these are highly modified instruments that help them eat their prey.
