Genetic diversity also helps animals withstand attacks from parasites. The more different genotypes there are in the population, the more difficult it is for a parasite to overwhelm the entire population. When each potential host it encounters has a different genotype, the parasite needs to come up with a different solution each time.

This situation often results in 'evolutionary arms races'. As the parasites get better at evading the hosts' defences, the host responds by improving the way it repels the parasite. This often leads to a stalemate where neither ever gets the upper hand.
