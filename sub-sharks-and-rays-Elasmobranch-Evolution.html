Elasmobranch Evolution

The first 'elasmobranchs' (sharks and rays) appeared about 400 million years ago in the Devonian Period on the geological time scale, which is also known as the 'age of fish'! The first elasmobranchs were prehistoric sharks and it wasn't until around 150 million years ago, in the late Jurassic Period, that the first rays appeared.

What you may not realise is that many prehistoric sharks became extinct along with the dinosaurs! The species we see today are the 'neoselachians' (the modern sharks and rays) which evolved about 200 million years ago. Of these modern sharks, there was one particular shark, a giant super-predator which the world's oceans had never seen before... Megalodon!
